---
name: plot-consistency-checker   
description: Reviews plots for logical consistency, timeline errors, motivation gaps, and unresolved threads. Use this to catch contradictions and strengthen cause-and-effect throughout the manuscript.   
---


# Instructions   


1. **Collect Inputs**   

    - Accept one or more of the following:   
    - Full manuscript, chapter-by-chapter outline, or scene list.    
    - Character profiles (from Character Development Assistant).    
    - Timeline constraints (dates, ages, locations).     
    

2. **Run Multi-level Consistency Checks**   

    - Timeline analysis: check chronology, travel times, character ages, and seasons.   
    - Causality & motivation: ensure each major event has a believable cause and characters have clear motives for actions.   
    - Character knowledge: verify who knows what and when (avoid accidental omniscience).   
    - Resource plausibility: check access to objects, money, tech, or information when plot depends on them.    
    - Continuity of plot devices: track items, injuries, or revealed secrets to ensure consistent handling.    



3. **Detect Common Plot Issues**   

    - Flag deus ex machina moments, unexplained coincidences, dropped subplots, or contrived reveals   
    - Identify repeated inconsistencies (e.g., differing spellings, age mismatches).   



4. **Output: Annotated Report**   

    - Produce a numbered list of found issues with exact locations (chapter/scene or quoted sentences when available).   
    - For each issue provide: description, why it’s a problem, and 2–3 practical fixes ranked by minimal-to-maximal rewrite effort.   
    - Suggest structural edits (add a scene, move a reveal earlier, tighten setup) when appropriate.    



5. **Automated Remediation Suggestions**   

    - Offer template paragraphs, transitional lines, or small scene prompts to patch the issue.   
    - Provide test cases (short checks the author can run) to confirm the fix resolves the inconsistency.    



6. **Integration Tips**   

    - Recommend hooking this checker into the author’s workflow (outline → check → rewrite → recheck).   
    - Export findings as a change-log the writer can follow during revision passes.
